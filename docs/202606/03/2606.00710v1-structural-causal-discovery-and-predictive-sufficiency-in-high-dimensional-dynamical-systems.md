## 摘要
高维环境系统通常包含强预测性、结构信息丰富且物理耦合的变量，但这些角色并不等价。在降水动力学中，这种区别尤为重要，因为降雨源于多尺度热力学、运动学、微物理学和陆-气相互作用，而观测数据稀疏、空间冗余且严重不平衡。本研究探讨高维降水系统中结构因果发现与预测充分性之间的关系。利用美国西南部的HRRR大气场和MRMS降水观测，我们应用投影形式的熵回归来识别下一小时降水的候选因果父变量。该方法通过变量在一小时时间延迟下的增量条件信息贡献进行评估，并利用空间聚合的超像素表示来评估整个区域的结构一致性。与传递熵和因果熵相比，熵回归产生了更集中且稳定的选择轮廓，揭示了与水分可用性、反射率、垂直运动、风组织和对流不稳定性相关的六个可物理解释的紧凑变量集。仅使用这些结构选择的变量进行的预测实验显示出对降水发生的强短时区分能力，但校准、强度预测和固定阈值事件检测技能有限。这些结果表明，结构相关性并不意味着预测封闭性。所选变量构成了降水过程的稳定信息骨架，但并不构成完整的预测状态。

## Abstract
High-dimensional environmental systems often contain variables that are strongly predictive, structurally informative, and physically coupled, but these roles are not equivalent. In precipitation dynamics, this distinction is particularly important because rainfall emerges from multiscale thermodynamic, kinematic, microphysical, and land--atmosphere interactions, while observations are sparse, spatially redundant, and strongly imbalanced. In this work, we study the relationship between structural causal discovery and predictive sufficiency in a high-dimensional precipitation system. Using HRRR atmospheric fields and MRMS precipitation observations over the Southwestern United States, we apply a projection-based formulation of entropic regression to identify candidate causal parents of next-hour precipitation. The method evaluates variables through their incremental conditional information contribution under a one-hour temporal delay, using spatially aggregated superpixel representations to assess structural consistency across the domain. Compared with transfer entropy and causation entropy, entropic regression produces a more concentrated and stable selection profile, revealing a compact set of six physically interpretable variables associated with moisture availability, reflectivity, vertical motion, wind organization, and convective instability. Predictive experiments using only these structurally selected variables show strong short-horizon discrimination of precipitation occurrence, but limited calibration, intensity prediction, and fixed-threshold event-detection skill. These results demonstrate that structural relevance does not imply predictive closure. The selected variables form a stable informational backbone of the precipitation process, but they do not constitute a complete predictive state.
